California Legal Standards

California has some of the strongest Disability related protections in the US. Not only does California follow the Americans with Disabilities Act, there have also been additional regulations and acts passed that further bolster and increase the protections for individuals with disabilities. Below are summarized examples of how California furthers the protections for individuals with disabilities:

California Unruh Civil Rights Act

The Unruh Civil Rights Act adds additional protections from discrimination by all business establishments in the state of California and encompases housing and public accommodations. The protected categories include but aren't necessarily limited to Age, Ancestry, Color, Race, Disability, National Origin, Religion, Sex and Sexual Orientation.

Title V Regulations for DSPS Programs

Title V regulations are California's guiding policies and procedures for Disabled Student Programs and Services (DSPS) at California Colleges and Universities. Title V provides guidance on a variety of topics including but not limited to Categories of Disabilities, Disability Examples, Funding Guidelines, and much more. These regulations are published by the California Community Colleges Chancellors Office and a copy can be found on the State Center Community College Website.
