What is Alternative Media?

Alternative media is a selection of media formats that can be created so that they are accessible to individuals with disabilities (or any individual). Some of the common types are listed below:

Audio

Audio is a very common format in daily life. Many individuals who don't necessarily have a disability use audio related items like "audio books" which also heavily benefit individuals who are low vision or blind, and it can even assist individuals with ADHD who struggle to focus on a page or lose track of where they are easily.

Braille

Braille was created by Louie Braille who was an individual that was blind. Louie worked to find a format and method of writing and communication that would be easy to teach, interpret, and create. You will commonly see examples of Braille on Classroom or Department door plaques.

Tactile Graphics

Tactile graphics tend to go hand in hand with Braille. Essentially tactile graphics are "touch" graphics that specialists can create either by using a premade template or by creating it manually. Many of these files can be created using Braille Embossers and specialized software. Different items can be recreated utilizing a mix of Braille and Tactile Graphics like graphs, charts, pie charts, anatomy images (long story), and much more.
