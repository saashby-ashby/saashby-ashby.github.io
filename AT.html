What is Assistive Technology?

Assistive Technology is technology that is designed or implemented to assist individuals with disabilities be able to function independantly in daily life functions. There are both Low Tech and High Tech options available, and options can range from a simple communication board or communication cards to a tablet that has specialized communication software that allows an individual to communicate without the use of their voice. Below are some of the common categories of Assistive Technology and at the very end is a table that lists actual programs and resources that are examples of the various resources that fit into this area of AT.

TTS or Text-To-Speech Programs and Software

TTS programs are designed to take text froma document or file and convert it to speech so that the individual can be able to audibly listen to the file. This is commonly utilized by individuals who struggle to look at text for long periods of time and also can be used by individuals that struggle with Vision loss.

Dictation or Speech to Text Programs and Software

Dictation refers to the ability to verbally speak and depending on the program possibly even control their laptop hands free. This is beneficial for individuals who are unable to write for long periods of time or indviduals who may have lost functionality due to their disability.

AAC or Augmentive and Alternative Communication

AAC is a category of programs and software that assist individuals who are either unable to speak or have periods of time where they are unable to communicate or even struggle to communicate. AAC can range from low tech such as pictures of different needs or actions on a board that the individual can point at, to high tech options like tablets with specialized software that allow for the individuals to tap on the screen or utilize eye tracking software to select which option they want to have a synthesized voice read aloud. A benefit of this type of program is that it can be highly customized to the users needs.

Job Access With Speech (JAWS) Screen Reader Software JAWS is a screen reader program that allows for individuals that are low vision or blind to be able to indepenently navigate technology without the need of a third party. JAWS essentially will read outloud what is on the users screen as long as it is accessible. If what the user is attempting to view or listen to is inaccessible things become very complicated as screen reader software heavily relies on whether or not the program, website, or content was coded correctly and designed with accessibility in mind. Vispero JAWS Screen Reader Website
Kurzweil 3000 Text to Speech Kurzweil 3000 is a powerful reading, writing, studying and test taking tool that is build on the basis of UDL which allows curriculum to be accessible to all students. Kurzweil is highly customizable to the individuals preferences and needs which then in turn can assist with learning retention and better understanding of the content. Kurzweil Education Website
ZoomText Screen Enlargement ZoomText is screen magnification that allows for individuals with low vision to enlarge the content on their screen past the system default settings. Depending on the version it also has the ability to read aloud to the individual what is on the screen such as app/program names, website shortcut names, and more. Vispero ZoomText Screen Magnification Site
Dragon Naturally Speaking Dictation Dragon Naturally Speaking is designed to assist individuals with being able to verbally dictate documents and much more. With Dragon you also have the ability to remotely control your device/computer without physically interacting with the device. Dragon Naturally Speaking Nuance Site
